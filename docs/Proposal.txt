We propose a project on graph and geometric deep learning for predicting whether a small molecule binds to a specific protein target. The idea is to study whether molecular graph representations, and potentially 3D geometry-aware models, can improve target-specific binding prediction compared to more classical machine learning baselines. This is a timely research question, since recent literature continues to highlight challenges in generalization and the effective use of richer molecular representations in drug-related prediction tasks. The project will focus on a selected protein target in order to keep the scope manageable. Our objective is to conduct a comparative study between traditional molecular descriptors, graph neural networks, and possibly geometric deep learning architectures, and to evaluate whether structural and geometric information provides a meaningful advantage in this pharmaceutical prediction task.
